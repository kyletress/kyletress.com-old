Me in 30 seconds

I’m an olympic athlete, writer, programmer, and thinker.

I’m fascinated with flourishing, a concept from positive psychology that means living within the optimal range of human existence. I approach it from an interdisciplinary standpoint, combining ideas from the fields of performance, philosophy, pyschology, neuroscience, computation, and future studies.

I try to keep my identity small. Minimalism, stoicism and humanism are the philosophies that best capture my personality. INTP.

Here’s what I’m doing right now →

Skeleton

I’ve been an athlete on the United States National skeleton team since 2002. In February, I competed in the 2014 Winter Olympic Games in Sochi and I’m currently training for the 2018 Winter Olympic Games in PyeongChang, Korea.

In addition to being an athlete, I am the founder of Sledsheet, a database of skeleton results dating back to 2004. I also publish a list of frequently asked questions about skeleton largely written for new sliders.

Reading

I’m an avid reader. I make weekly videos about books on my YouTube channel, and once a month I send out a recommended reading email featuring 3-5 favorite books. You can see the big list of books I’m reading, and my commonplace book while you’re there.

Writing

I write personal essays on this site under the loose topic of flourishing. My writing touches on performance, philosophy, reading, future studies, and other interests. One of my personal writing goals this year is to sell my first book, about the philosophy of Stoicism for athletes and other high performers.

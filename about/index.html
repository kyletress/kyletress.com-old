About

Hi, I’m Kyle Tress, an Olympic athlete, writer, coder, and futurist. This is Meditations, my personal website, where I write about skeleton, books, and other things that interest me. You can learn more about the site here.

In 2002 at the age of 21, I saw the sport of skeleton for the first time. Many years, dollars, and injuries later I’m ranked 3rd in the country and 12th in the world. In February 2014, I had the honor of representing the United States at the Winter Olympic Games in Sochi. You can read more about that experience on my skeleton page.

When I’m not racing headfirst down a mountain of ice at 90 miles per hour, I enjoy reading, writing, and coding. I believe code is the Lingua Franca of the modern world, and literacy (not fluency), should be taught in every primary school. I occasionally design and develop my own apps, including an iPad app featured by Apple and Creative Arts Magazine.

Coding is a hobby, but writing is a calling. I write 1000 words a day, 6 days per week, a practice I have adhered to for many years.

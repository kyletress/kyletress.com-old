About Kyle

Hi I’m Kyle. I’m an Olympic athlete in the sport of skeleton. When I’m not racing headfirst down a mountain of ice at 90 miles per hour, I like to read, write, and code.

I tweet from @kyletress, share photos on instagram, and make videos on YouTube. You can email me at kyle@kyletress.com.

Here’s what I’m focused on right now →

Skeleton

I’ve been an athlete on the United States National skeleton team since 2002. In February 2014, I competed in the Winter Olympic Games in Sochi and I’m currently training for the 2018 Winter Olympic Games in PyeongChang, Korea. I’m ranked 2nd in the United States and 17th in the world. You can learn more about my skeleton career on my skeleton page.

In addition to being an athlete, I am the creator of Sledsheet, a database of skeleton results dating back to 2004. I also publish a list of frequently asked questions here on my site.

Work

I’ve been fascinated with technology ever since I built my first website in 1994. I’ve worked with many different languages, tools, and technologies over the years, but today I’m most interested in front-end development, Ruby on Rails, UI/UX design, and iOS development. Former clients include Capital Health System New Jersey, Design by Diane Alder, USA Bobsled and Skeleton, and Nature of Speed, LLC.

Life

Aside from skeleton and technology, I’m primarily interested in flourishing, a concept from the field of positive psychology that means to live within the optimal range of human existence. I approach flourishing from an interdisciplinary standpoint by conducting personal research in fields like performance, philosophy, psychology, neuroscience, computation, and future studies.

I try to keep my identity small. Philosophically I am influenced by both Epicureanism and Stoicism. I am a secular humanist, a minimalist, and an INTP.

This site

This site is named after one of my favorite books, Meditations by Marcus Aurelius. It’s minimal by design, as I believe personal websites are the autobiographies of the digital age and should reflect their owner’s personality.

The site is powered by Siteleaf and hosted on GitHub Pages. You can view the source code on GitHub. All photos and content are mine unless otherwise noted. My headshot is courtesy of Damian Battinelli.

I work on a MacBook Air. My development setup is cloud-based (Nitrous.io). For visual design I use Sketch. I have written more about my setup here.

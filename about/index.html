About

Hi, I’m Kyle Tress. I’m an Olympic athlete in the sport of skeleton and this is my website. I call it Meditations after my favorite book. Here I write personal essays, recommend great books, and share updates on my skeleton career.

In 2002 at the age of 21, I saw the sport of skeleton for the first time. Many years, dollars, and injuries later I’m ranked 3rd in the country and 12th in the world. In February 2014, I had the honor of representing the United States at the Winter Olympic Games in Sochi. You can read more about that experience on my skeleton page.

When I’m not racing headfirst down a mountain of ice at 90 miles per hour, I enjoy reading, writing, and coding. I believe code is the Lingua Franca of the modern world, and literacy (not fluency), should be taught in every primary school. I occasionally design and develop my own apps, including an iPad app featured by Apple and Creative Arts Magazine.

Coding is a hobby, but writing is a calling. I write 1000 words a day, 6 days per week, a practice I have adhered to for many years.

Want to know more? Email me.

This site

My site is named after one of my favorite books, Meditations by Marcus Aurelius. It’s minimal by design, as I believe personal websites are the autobiographies of the digital age and should reflect their owner’s personality.

The site is powered by Siteleaf and hosted on GitHub Pages. You can view the source code on GitHub. All photos and content are mine unless otherwise noted. My headshot is courtesy of Damian Battinelli.

I work on a MacBook Air. For visual design I use Sketch and I connect to a Nitrous.io box. I have written more about my setup here.

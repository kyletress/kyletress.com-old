Is skeleton scary?

Probably not in the way that most people think. I’m never scared to race down the track headfirst at autobahn speeds. Even tracks with notorious reputations, like Whistler or Altenberg, are fun and challenging in a good way. The first run of the season down each track brings some butterflies, but that’s normal for most competitors. While there is always the possiblity of injury (or worse), that’s true of many sports and life in general.

The things that do scare me are largely outside my control. The scariest scenario for me is probably hitting something in the track. This includes people, animals (it’s happened before) or tools that aren’t supposed to be there. The time to react to this very small, and a headfirst collision at those speeds can be fatal.

TBI is also a growing area of concern. In light of recent studies and revelations from a few former athletes, I’ve begun to wonder about the long term effects of skeleton sliding. Our rides are rarely smooth. There is a lot of vibration, the possibility of concussion, and occasional impact with the ice. Concussions aren’t common, but they occur. This is something to keep an eye on.

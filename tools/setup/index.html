The Setup

I’m Kyle Tress, an Olympic athlete, writer, and software developer. I race headfirst down a mountain of ice at 90 miles per hour.

What hardware are you using?

I’m an Apple guy. I write and code on an 11" MacBook Air, which is the best computer I’ve ever owned.

My iPad Air 2 is my primary device. I use it for everything, including design and development. Sometimes I pair it with a Wireless Apple Keyboard and a 12South compass stand.

My iPhone 6 is an extension of my brain and I never leave home without it. I use it to capture ideas, browse the web, shoot 1 second videos, and update social media. It’s my primary camera because I always have it with me. I’m also really impressed with Apple Pay and I’m eagerly awaiting the day when it can completely replace my wallet.

I’m an avid reader. I cut cable to make more time for the unread books on my Kindle Voyage, but I still enjoy Netflix and Hulu Plus on my AppleTV. I have a Wii U for the occasional Zelda/Mario Kart fix.

I’m on the road 6 months per year. I love airplanes almost as much as I love Apple, but I couldn’t survive a long flight without my Bose QuietComfort headphones. While traveling, essentials get packed away in my Scottevest Revolution, and the rest goes in my Tom Bihn aeronaut and copilot. I throw in a portable coffee kit consisting of an AeroPress, Hario Grinder, and an electric travel kettle and I’m all set to make excellent coffee wherever my sled takes me.

And what software?

I write long form content in Scrivner. I used MarsEdit to manage my personal website until I switched from Wordpress to Siteleaf. Now I write Markdown in IA Writer on the Mac and Editorial on my iPad.

Atom is my code editor of choice, but these days I mostly connect to a Nitrous.io box. It gives me the freedom to code from anywhere I can get a cell signal. I use iTerm2 when doing command line work.

I prefer Safari for daily browsing, but keep Chrome around for the occasional flash video (why won’t you die already?). I couldn’t imagine life without 1password, and integration with iOS 8 extensions has made it much more convenient.

I use too many iOS apps to mention, but a few of my favorites are: Mailbox, Flipboard, Netflix, NFL GamePass, Editorial, Diet Coda, Day One, Sky Guide, Prompt, and GoodReader.

Finally, I back everything up to the cloud. My code lives on GitHub, and of course I use iCloud. The fewer items on my local computer, the better.

What would be your dream setup?

I’m happy with my current setup, but I’m interested in the rumors surrounding the 12" MacBook Pro. I’m also intrigued by the Apple Watch not just as a user, but a developer as well.

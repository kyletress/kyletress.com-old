The Setup

I’m Kyle Tress, an Olympic athlete, writer, and software developer. I race headfirst down a mountain of ice at 90 miles per hour.

What hardware are you using?

I’m an Apple guy. I write and code on an 11" MacBook Air, which is the best computer I’ve ever owned. I also have a 2009 iMac that’s seen better days.

My Retina iPad Mini is my primary device. I use it for everything, including development. Sometimes I’ll pair it with a Wireless Apple Keyboard and a 12South compass stand.

My iPhone 5 is an extension of my brain and I never leave home without it. I use it to capture ideas, search the web, shoot 1 second videos, and update social media. It’s my primary camera because I always have with me. The battery is terrible and I rarely call anyone - but damn, I love it.

I’m an avid reader. I cut cable to make more time for the unread books on my Kindle Paperwhite, but I still enjoy Netflix and Hulu Plus on my AppleTV. I have a Wii U for the occasional Zelda/Mario Kart fix.

I’m on the road 6 months per year. I love airplanes almost as much as I love Apple, but I couldn’t survive a long flight without my Bose QuietComfort headphones. While traveling, essentials get packed away in my Scottevest Revolution, and the rest goes in my Tom Bihn aeronaut and copilot. I throw in a portable coffee kit consisting of an AeroPress, Hario Grinder, and an electric travel kettle and I’m all set to make excellent coffee wherever my sled takes me.

And what software?

I write long form content in Scrivner. I used MarsEdit to manage my personal website until I switched from Wordpress to Siteleaf. Now I write Markdown in IA Writer on the Mac and Editorial on my iPad.

Atom is my code editor of choice, but these days I mostly connect to a Nitrous.io box. It gives me the freedom to code from anywhere I can get a cell signal. I use iTerm2 when doing command line work.

I prefer Safari for daily browsing, but keep Chrome around for the occasional flash video (why won’t you die already!). I couldn’t imagine life without 1password, and I can’t wait for better integration in iOS 8 via extensions.

I use too many iOS apps to mention, but a few of my favorites are: Mailbox, Flipboard, Netflix, NFL Sunday Ticket, Editorial, Diet Coda, Day One, Sky Guide, Prompt, Screens, and GoodReader. The last three ensure I can view, access, and control my iMac wherever I am in the world.

Finally, I back everything up to the cloud. My entire hard drive is backed up to BackBlaze. My code lives on GitHub, my torrents live on a private server, and of course I use iCloud. The fewer items on my local computer, the better.

What would be your dream setup?

Pursuit of the next thing is largely hollow, so I try to temper my desire for new things by enjoying what I already have. It’s a work in progress.

I think I’d be more efficient with a 12" MacBook Pro, and I’d like a 5.5" iPhone. An iWatch would probably make a terrific training tool.

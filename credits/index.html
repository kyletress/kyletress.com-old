This site is named after one of my favorite books, Meditations by Marcus Aurelius. It’s simple and minimal by design. I believe personal websites are the autobiographies of the digital age and should reflect the interests of their owners. Therefore you can find a lot of interesting stuff here – if you dig a little.

Meditations is built on Siteleaf and hosted on GitHub Pages. The site is version controlled with Git and the source is viewable on GitHub.

All photos are mine unless otherwise noted. My headshot is courtesy of Damian Battinelli.

The font is FF Meta Web Pro delivered by TypeKit. If it’s sans-serif, it’s probably Source Sans Pro.

I use a variety of tools. For visual design I use Sketch. Atom is my editor of choice, but these days I mostly connect to a Nitrous.io box. This gives me the freedom to work from anywhere my iPad gets cellular service. I like to work on the command line, so iTerm2 and Prompt round out my toolbox. For the gearheads out there, check out the rest of my setup.

The site makes use of CSS3 features like Flexbox, transitions, and transforms. Therefore it is optimized for IE10 and above, but the simple design translates pretty well across browsers.

Made on a Mac.

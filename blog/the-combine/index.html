The combine

The combine is a mandatory event for all bobsled/skeleton athletes. It’s a test of speed, power, and explosiveness, not unlike the NFL combine. The 8 events include 15m, 30m, 45m, and flying 30m sprints, followed by broad jump, underhand shot toss, power clean, and back squat. Maximum score is 800 points, and a passing score is 650.

Here is a table of my combine score progression for the past three seasons. While I have great scores in the lifting and power events, my lack of track and field experience shows in the sprint portion. However I seem to be an anomaly when it comes the pushing/sprinting correlation in skeleton. Despite having one of the slower 30m times on the team, I have one of the faster starts.

I’m happy with my performance this year. I had great improvements in my power events. I added 10cm to my broad jump, threw the shot 1.5 meters farther, and improved both back squat and power clean lifts. I do want to focus on sprint training to bring those numbers to higher standard, but at this point in my career it’s not a major concern. Push time and velocity are far more important.

Next up is Push Championships on September 27th, followed by Team Trials in the fall. We’re getting close now!

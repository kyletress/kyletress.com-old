Should NBC show Nodar's crash?

In short — absolutely. It was the right journalistic decision. I’ve heard the argument that NBC and the media have a responsibility to Nodar’s family, and should show sensitivity and restrain during a time like this. That’s only partially correct. They must be sensitive to the family’s needs. However their responsibility is first and foremost to report the news, particularly significant stories such as this. Would we as a country ever tolerate the censorship of the 9/11 footage? If not that, why this?

To me, the footage represents a tribute to an athlete who died doing what he loved. If we are to learn from Nodar’s death, it must be viewed as an agent of positive change in both our sport and our approach to track design and construction. I hope I never see the footage again. However showing it was a necessary decision and I agree with it completely.

Update: Yahoo! Sports has an article about this topic, which I’ve linked to at the bottom of the page. I want to highlight a brief passage from the article here.

But before we vilify NBC, let’s recognize that they were in a difficult spot. If they didn’t show the video, people would be complaining that the network was protecting its asset (the Olympics) and not providing detailed coverage of an important story. That wouldn’t be gratuitous, it would be bad journalism. By showing it the opposite argument is made. NBC was right to cover the story and showing the video was an acceptable news decision. However, showing it three times at the beginning of coverage and without an adequate warning was irresponsible.

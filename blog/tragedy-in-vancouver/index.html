Tragedy in Vancouver

This morning, 21-year old Georgian Luge athlete Nodar Kumaritashvili was killed on the Olympic track in Whistler.

A men’s Olympic luger from the country of Georgia died Friday after a high-speed crash on a track that is the world’s fastest and has raised safety concerns among competitors. A tearful IOC president Jacques Rogge said the death hours before the opening ceremony “clearly casts a shadow over these games.”

If we can learn anything from this tragedy, I hope it’s the realization that we have a responsibility to speak up when we have concerns. I’m concerned over speed and design issues on the Whistler track. I’m concerned with the notion of preserving “home track advantage”, which prevents smaller nations from getting adequate training time. Most importantly, like everyone else, I’m angry and saddened that a 21-year old slider died today participating in a sport he loved.

We all know the risks involved when we step on the track, but it’s easy to become complacent. I can only hope this opens up healthy, rational debate about the changes that must be made to ensure our safety as we move forward.
